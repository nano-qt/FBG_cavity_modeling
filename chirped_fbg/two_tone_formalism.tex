\section{Goal}



For robust phase extraction, here we consider the passively stable scheme, without using the Michelson Interferometer (requiring thermal stability).
Given the single-frequency FBG response $r(\delta)$ from the transfer-matrix
simulator, reconstruct both the amplitude and the phase of the grating
response from a \emph{realistic} dual-sideband (EOM) measurement at a
finite modulation frequency $\Delta$.

\begin{figure}[h]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=0.7\linewidth]{fig/schematics_chirp.png}
  \caption{Schematic of characterizing phase shift of the chirped FBG. }
  \label{fig:schematic_chirp}
\end{figure}
\section{Observable}

With EOM phase modulation at $\Delta$ and modulation index $m$, I/Q
demodulation of the photodetector output at frequency $\Delta$ yields the
complex envelope
\begin{equation}
    \boxed{\;
    \mathcal{C}(\delta;\Delta)
    \;\equiv\; r^*(\delta)\,r(\delta+\Delta)
    \;-\; r(\delta)\,r^*(\delta-\Delta),
    \;}
    \label{eq:C}
\end{equation}
up to the overall factor $|E_0|^2\, J_0(m)\, J_1(m)$. The derivation is in
Appendix~\ref{app:derivation}. The experimentally measured amplitude is
\begin{equation}
    |S_{\mathrm{LI}}(\delta;\Delta)|
    \;=\; |E_0|^2\, J_0(m)\, J_1(m)\, |\mathcal{C}(\delta;\Delta)|.
\end{equation}

\section{Experimental procedure}
\label{sec:procedure}

\begin{figure}[h]
  \centering
  % NOTE: `#` in filenames can break `\includegraphics` (it uses `\edef` internally).
  \includegraphics[width=0.8\linewidth]{fig/measurement_007.png}
  \caption{Simulated four-stage measurement at $m = 1.8412$ (first zero of
  $J_1'$, i.e.\ maximum of $J_1 \approx 0.582$) and
  $\Delta = 100\,\mathrm{MHz}$: (1) triangular laser-frequency sweep
  $\delta(t)$; (2) DC reflected $R$ and transmitted $T$ powers with the EOM
  on, log scale; (3) same DC reflection together with the I/Q demod
  amplitude $J_0 J_1\,|\mathcal{C}|$ on the same log axis, now fairly
  comparable in units; (4) reconstructed phase $\widehat\phi(t)$ built by
  integrating the symmetric finite-difference estimator
  $\arg[r(\delta+\Delta)/r(\delta-\Delta)]/(2\Delta)$ along the sweep
  trajectory.}
  \label{fig:measurement}
\end{figure}

The four operational steps (matching the four panels of
Fig.~\ref{fig:measurement}):

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Sweep.} Triangular detuning $\delta(t)$ across the FBG band.
  Sweep period ($\sim$ms) is many decades slower than $1/\Delta = 10\,\mathrm{ns}$,
  so the RF response is quasi-static at every time point.

  \item \textbf{Detect DC.} The low-pass photocurrent with modulation on is
  \begin{equation}
      R(\delta) \;=\; \sum_{k} J_k(m)^2\,|r(\delta+k\Delta)|^2,
      \qquad
      T(\delta) \;=\; \sum_{k} J_k(m)^2\,|t(\delta+k\Delta)|^2.
      \label{eq:DC_mod}
  \end{equation}
  For $\Delta$ much smaller than the FBG features,
  $R(\delta) \approx |r(\delta)|^2$ and $T(\delta) \approx |t(\delta)|^2$.

  \item \textbf{Demodulate at $\Delta$.} Mix the photocurrent with an
  electrical reference at $\Delta$ and low-pass: the complex envelope is
  $|E_0|^2 J_0 J_1\,\mathcal{C}$. The panel plots its magnitude,
  $|J_0 J_1\,\mathcal{C}|$, so it is in the same units as $R$ and $T$
  (dimensionless, $|r|^2$-like).

  \item \textbf{Reconstruct phase.} From the complex I/Q output, extract the
  symmetric finite-difference estimator
  \begin{equation}
      \widehat{\phi'}(\delta)
      \;=\; \frac{\arg r(\delta+\Delta) - \arg r(\delta-\Delta)}{2\,\Delta},
      \qquad
      \widehat{\phi}(\delta)
      \;=\; \int_{\delta_0}^{\delta} \widehat{\phi'}(\delta')\,d\delta'.
      \label{eq:phi_recon}
  \end{equation}
  Eq.~\eqref{eq:phi_recon} requires access to the individual sideband
  phases, which I/Q demodulation provides through the coherent reference.
  Leading-order error is
  $\tfrac{1}{6}\Delta^2\,\phi'''(\delta) + O(\Delta^4)$
  (see Appendix~\ref{app:smalldelta}).
\end{enumerate}

\section{Result: spectrum view}
\label{sec:spectrum}

\begin{figure}[h]
\centering\includegraphics[width=0.7\linewidth]{fig/spectrum_007.png}
  \caption{Transmission $|t|^2$ and demod amplitude
  $J_0 J_1\,|\mathcal{C}|$ on log scale (left axis) and reconstructed phase
  $\widehat{\phi}/\pi$ (right axis) as functions of detuning, all obtained
  from a single $\Delta = 100\,\mathrm{MHz}$.}
  \label{fig:spectrum}
\end{figure}

Remapping the time traces onto the frequency axis gives the FBG response in
the usual spectroscopic form. Visible features:

\begin{itemize}\setlength\itemsep{2pt}
  \item The $\sim 100\,\mathrm{GHz}$-wide reflection band appears as the
        rejection in $|t|^2$ down to $\sim 10^{-2}$, with characteristic
        ripple from the finite chirped length.
  \item The diffenretial amplitude $J_0 J_1\,|\mathcal{C}|$ exhibits the expected dispersive structure and modulation across the band.
  \item The reconstructed phase $\widehat\phi$ accumulates roughly $50\pi$ across the band, consistent with the long chirped structure and with the exact $\arg r$ from the transfer-matrix calculation.
\end{itemize}

Because Eq.~\eqref{eq:phi_recon} is a symmetric finite difference, the
systematic error is $\tfrac{1}{6}\Delta^2\,\phi'''(\delta) + O(\Delta^4)$:
for $\Delta = 100\,\mathrm{MHz}$ vs. an $\sim 100\,\mathrm{GHz}$ FBG scale,
the error sits well below the plot resolution.

\appendix

\section{Derivation of $\mathcal{C}$ (dual-sideband EOM)}
\label{app:derivation}

\subsection{Input field}

A CW laser at $\omega_c$ phase-modulated by an EOM at $\Delta$, in the
slowly-varying envelope frame:
\begin{equation}
    E_{\mathrm{in}}(t) \;=\; E_0\, e^{\,i m \sin(2\pi\Delta t)}
    \;=\; E_0 \sum_{k=-\infty}^{\infty} J_k(m)\, e^{\,i 2\pi k\Delta t}.
\end{equation}
Keeping $k \in \{-1,0,+1\}$ and using $J_{-1}(m) = -J_1(m)$,
\begin{equation}
    E_{\mathrm{in}}(t) \;\approx\; E_0\!\left[J_0 + J_1\,e^{\,i 2\pi\Delta t} - J_1\,e^{-i 2\pi\Delta t}\right].
\end{equation}
The carrier is at detuning $\delta$ from the FBG resonance; the sidebands
are at $\delta\pm\Delta$.

\subsection{Reflected field and photodetection}

Linearity of the FBG gives
\begin{equation}
    E_r(t) \;=\; E_0\!\left[J_0\,r(\delta)
      + J_1\,r(\delta+\Delta)\,e^{\,i 2\pi\Delta t}
      - J_1\,r(\delta-\Delta)\,e^{-i 2\pi\Delta t}\right].
\end{equation}
Writing $A = J_0\,r(\delta)$, $B = J_1\,r(\delta+\Delta)$,
$C = -J_1\,r(\delta-\Delta)$ and computing $|E_r|^2/|E_0|^2$:
\begin{equation}
    \frac{P(t)}{|E_0|^2}
    = \underbrace{|A|^2+|B|^2+|C|^2}_{\text{DC}}
    + \left[\,(A C^* + B A^*)\,e^{\,i 2\pi\Delta t} + \text{c.c.}\,\right]
    + \left[\,B C^*\,e^{\,i 4\pi\Delta t} + \text{c.c.}\,\right].
\end{equation}
The beat at $\Delta$ has complex coefficient
\begin{equation}
    A C^* + B A^*
    = J_0 J_1\!\left[r^*(\delta)\,r(\delta+\Delta) - r(\delta)\,r^*(\delta-\Delta)\right]
    = J_0 J_1\,\mathcal{C}(\delta;\Delta),
\end{equation}
giving Eq.~\eqref{eq:C}.

\subsection{Demodulation}

Mixing $P(t)$ with $e^{-i 2\pi\Delta t}$ and low-passing,
\begin{equation}
    \left\langle P(t)\,e^{-i 2\pi\Delta t}\right\rangle_t
    \;=\; |E_0|^2\, J_0(m)\, J_1(m)\,\mathcal{C}(\delta;\Delta).
\end{equation}
Equivalently, a DFT of a simulated $P(t)$ at $+\Delta$ gives the same
$\mathcal{C}$ up to window factors.

\section{Small-$\Delta$ expansion and reconstruction error}
\label{app:smalldelta}

With $r(\delta) = R(\delta)\,e^{i\phi(\delta)}$ and
$r(\delta\pm\Delta) = r \pm \Delta r' + \tfrac12 \Delta^2 r'' + O(\Delta^3)$,
\begin{align}
    r^*(\delta)\,r(\delta+\Delta)
    &= |r|^2 + \Delta\, r^* r' + \tfrac{1}{2}\Delta^2\, r^* r'' + O(\Delta^3),\\
    r(\delta)\,r^*(\delta-\Delta)
    &= |r|^2 - \Delta\, r\, r'^{\,*} + \tfrac{1}{2}\Delta^2\, r\, r''^{\,*} + O(\Delta^3).
\end{align}
Subtracting and using $r^* r' = \tfrac{1}{2}\,d|r|^2/d\delta + i|r|^2\phi'$
and $\Im(r^* r'') = d(R^2\phi')/d\delta$:
\begin{equation}
    \mathcal{C}(\delta;\Delta)
    \;\approx\; \Delta\,\frac{d|r|^2}{d\delta}
    \;+\; i\,\Delta^2\,\frac{d(|r|^2\phi')}{d\delta}
    \;+\; O(\Delta^3).
    \label{eq:C_small}
\end{equation}
The parity $\mathcal{C}(-\Delta) = -\mathcal{C}^*(\Delta)$ fixes odd
$\Delta$ coefficients real and even ones imaginary.

\paragraph{Consequences.}
\begin{itemize}\setlength\itemsep{2pt}
  \item $\Re\mathcal{C}$ is leading in $\Delta$ and tracks $d|r|^2/d\nu$.
  \item $\Im\mathcal{C}$ is one order smaller and entangles $\phi'$ with the
        amplitude variation $R'/R$.
\end{itemize}
Hence extracting $d\phi/d\nu$ from $\mathcal{C}$ directly requires the
assumption $R' \ll R\phi'$. The symmetric finite-difference estimator in
Eq.~\eqref{eq:phi_recon} bypasses the issue by using the raw phases
$\arg r(\delta\pm\Delta)$ returned by the I/Q demodulator; its leading
error is $\tfrac{1}{6}\Delta^2\phi'''(\delta) + O(\Delta^4)$.

\section{Implementation notes}
\label{app:impl}

\begin{itemize}\setlength\itemsep{2pt}
  \item $r$ is sampled on a finite detuning grid; off-grid
        $r(\delta\pm\Delta)$ is obtained by \emph{polar} interpolation
        (magnitude and unwrapped phase separately). Linear Re/Im
        interpolation chords across the complex-plane arc between grid
        samples and produces spurious amplitude ripple at the grid rate.
  \item Scan $\delta \in [\delta_{\min} + |\Delta|,\; \delta_{\max} - |\Delta|]$
        so both sidebands stay in-band; truncate outside this range.
  \item The overall factor $|E_0|^2 J_0(m) J_1(m)$ is a scalar amplitude;
        only the $\delta$- and $\Delta$-dependence of $\mathcal{C}$ carries
        physics.
  \item $m = 1.8412$ (first zero of $J_1'$) maximizes $|J_1|\approx 0.582$.
        At this $m$ the higher-order sidebands ($J_2, J_3$) are
        non-negligible and the $\pm 1$-only truncation used in the derivation
        is an approximation. For a quantitative match include $\pm 2$
        sidebands explicitly in the beat expansion.
  \item The DC panel uses $\sum_k J_k^2\,|x|^2$ for both $R$ and $T$ so the
        photodetector level is correct at arbitrary $m$, independent of the
        $\pm 1$-only truncation used for the beat.
\end{itemize}
